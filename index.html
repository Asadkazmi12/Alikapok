Kapok

Kapok is a fluffy, silky yellowish fiber, similar in feel to milkweed or down. Kapok is similar to cotton in that both fibres are found around the plant seeds, rather than extracted from the stem or leaves. The hollow core makes kapok very light, 8 times lighter than cotton by volume. Kapok is chiefly cultivated in Asia; the kapok fibre important product of Pakistan
